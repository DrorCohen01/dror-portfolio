Work Experience

Pixel54 — Freelance UX/UI Designer

2015 — Present

Jerusalem, Israel

As a freelance UX/UI designer, I've had the privilege of working on a diverse range of projects across various industries. Each project brings unique challenges and new learning opportunities.

Landa Digital Printing — UX/UI Lead

Key Project

As UX/UI Lead at Landa, a pioneer in nano-technology digital printing, my focus was on transforming complex operational systems into clear, efficient and user-focused experiences. The work began with an in-depth analysis of real work processes and interaction patterns with the machines, which formed the basis for a modern and effective design strategy.

I led a collaborative process with engineers, developers and operations teams, continuously adapting the product experience to Landa's spirit of innovation. Projects included control interfaces, operational dashboards and smart monitoring tools.

Optimus — UX/UI Lead

Key Project

As UX/UI Lead at Optimus, a business management software, my task was to improve user engagement through intuitive and aesthetic design. The process began with analyzing user behavior, which formed the basis for a design strategy tailored to audience preferences.

Leading a collaborative team effort, we progressively improved user interfaces, adapting them to Optimus's innovative ethos.

CaloSense — UX/UI Designer

2020–2022

Tel Aviv, Israel

The challenge was to create a user-friendly health platform that presents complex health data and improves communication between patient and doctor. The process began by understanding goals, defining personas and mapping patient journeys.

During ideation I branched solutions, created wireframes and developed them into high-resolution prototypes with feedback from stakeholders and users. The result: a secure and sought-after RPM platform for managing the patient journey.

JpU — UX/UI Designer

2015–2019

Tel Aviv, Israel

I worked in close collaboration with JpU's product managers, in the specification and design of their platform, website and marketing materials.

The Challenge: Creating a web-based platform suitable for deployment on all M2M, C-IoT and NB-IoT platforms, with high scalability to support millions of devices.

The Result: Web platform, logo, brand identity, app interface, website, marketing materials and investor presentations. Since implementing the new brand identity, JpU has succeeded in significantly expanding its customer base worldwide.
